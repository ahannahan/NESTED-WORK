Kalam {theology}

kalām, in Islam, speculative theology. The term is derived from the phrase kalām Allāh (Arabic: “word of God”), which refers to the Qurʾān, the sacred scripture of Islam. Those who practice kalām are known as mutakallimūn. Related Topics: mutakallimūn.

Tafsir

tafsīr, (Arabic: “explanation,” “exegesis”) the science of explanation of the Qurʾān, the sacred scripture of Islam, or of Qurʾānic commentary. So long as Muhammad, the Prophet of Islam, was alive, no other authority for interpretations of the Qurʾānic revelations was recognized by Muslims

fiqh {Islamic jurisprudence}

Fiqh is Islamic jurisprudence. Fiqh is often described as the human understanding and practices of the sharia, that is human understanding of the divine Islamic law as revealed in the Quran and the Sunnah.
